# Privacy Policy for Sticky Tabs

Last Updated: May 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the "Sticky Tabs" Chrome extension handles your data. Our core philosophy is simple: your data belongs to you, and we have no interest in collecting, tracking, or sharing it.

## 1. Information Collection and Use

"Sticky Tabs" is designed to respect your privacy.

No Personal Data: The extension does not collect, log, or transmit any personally identifiable information.

No Data Collection: We do not track, store, or upload your browsing history, the URLs you visit, or your tab activity to any external servers.

Local Processing: Any data required for the extension to function—such as remembering which tabs you have designated as "sticky"—is stored entirely on your local device using Google Chrome's local storage APIs. This data never leaves your computer.

## 2. Third-Party Services, Ads, and Tracking

**No Advertisements:** "Sticky Tabs" does not display, inject, or serve advertisements.

**No Analytics:** We do not use third-party analytics tools, tracking pixels, or telemetry to monitor how you use the extension.

## 3. Permissions Justification

To perform its core function, "Sticky Tabs" requires access to specific browser permissions. These are used strictly for local operations:

- `tabs` and `tabGroups`: Necessary to identify when a tab is closed and to automatically reopen it if it was marked as sticky.
- `storage`: Necessary to save your preferences and sticky states locally on your device.
- `contextMenus`: Necessary to show a context menu to interact with the extension.

## 4. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally to reflect minor changes in functionality or browser requirements. Any updates will be reflected by a change in the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.
